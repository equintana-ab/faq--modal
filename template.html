Choosing the right laptop depends on your needs and how you’ll use it.

  • For work or school: Focus on performance, portability, and battery life.
  • For gaming or creative work: Look for a powerful processor, dedicated graphics card, and a high-resolution display.
  • Casual use: Simple tasks, such as browsing and streaming, are easily handled by standard entry-level laptops.
  • SSD (Solid State Drive): Uses flash memory, offering much faster read/write speeds and boot times.
  • HDD (Hard Disk Drive): Uses spinning magnetic disks to store digital data. It works by reading and writing data magnetically—best for storing files and light tasks.
  • 256GB: Enough for storing documents, music, and a few videos and pictures.
  • 512GB: Able to store higher-quality photos, store movies, and have enough space to install video games.
  • 1TB: Great for installing heavy programs for video or photo editing, storing larger games, and larger photos/videos.
  • 2TB: If you store lots of high-res videos, raw photos, or large datasets.

Some laptops allow you to upgrade storage and RAM, while others have fixed memory. You may ask our Abenson Personal Shoppers before purchasing if you plan to upgrade in the future.

Earphones are small, portable audio devices worn inside or just outside your ears. They’re designed for everyday convenience—perfect for commuting, workouts, calls, and casual listening.

In-The-Ear (Earbuds): These sit just outside your ear canal. They are affordable and lightweight, making them easy to carry in your pocket. These earphones are best for casual listening and quick calls.

In-Ear-Canal (Insert Type / IEMs): These fit snugly inside your ear canal and usually come with soft silicone tips in different sizes. These offer excellent noise isolation and clear sound even at lower volumes. With a secure fit, they’re great for workouts and active movement.

Wired: Wired headphones plug directly into your device, delivering stable sound with no lag and no need to worry about charging. With strong, consistent audio quality, they’re reliable for gaming or long listening sessions—perfect if you want simple, dependable performance anytime.

Wireless: Wireless headphones connect through Bluetooth or NFC (Near Field Communication), giving you more freedom to move. These are perfect for commuting, workouts, and multitasking. Ideal if convenience and mobility are your priorities.

Understanding a few basic specs can help you choose better:

Driver Size (mm):

The driver is the part that produces sound. Bigger drivers usually mean deeper bass and fuller sound.

Frequency Response (Hz):

This shows the range of sound the earphones can produce—from deep bass to high notes. A wider range means more detail in your music.

Sensitivity (dB/mW):

This tells you how loud the earphones can get with less power. Higher sensitivity means louder sound without draining your phone battery quickly.

Impedance (Ω):

This affects compatibility with your device. Low impedance works best with smartphones and tablets, while high impedance is typically designed for professional or high-end audio equipment.

NFC (Near Field Communication) makes connecting your wireless earphones faster and easier. Instead of going into Bluetooth settings, you simply tap your devices together.

To connect using NFC, simply select your music on your phone, then tap your earphones to your phone where the NFC logos are located. The devices will automatically pair and connect via Bluetooth.

Why It's Useful:
  • No need to manually search for your device
  • Faster pairing
  • Convenient when switching between devices
  • Ideal for on-the-go use

Bone conduction headphones transmit sound through vibrations in your cheekbones, rather than directly into your ears. Your ears stay open while you listen.

Benefits:
  • Stay Aware – You can still hear traffic or conversations.
  • Comfortable for Workouts – No pressure inside the ear canal.
  • More Hygienic – No ear blockage.
  • Helpful for Some Hearing Conditions – Suitable for people with conductive hearing loss.
  • Underwater Use: Some models are designed for swimming, such as IP68-rated earbuds. Perfect for runners, cyclists, and outdoor users who need awareness.
