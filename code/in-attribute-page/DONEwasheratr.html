Frequently Asked Questions

Here are quick answers to questions shoppers ask the most.

  • Top Load Washing Machine: Clothes are loaded from the top. Easier to load and unload; generally more affordable; faster wash cycles.
  • Front Load Washing Machine: Clothes are loaded from the front. More energy- and water-efficient; gentler on clothes; better wash quality.
  • Combo Washer & Dryer: Front load washing machine with drying capabilities in the same drum, eliminating the need for a separate dryer. Excellent space-saving option.
  • Twin Tub: Basic washing machine with 2 tubs. One for washing, and the other for drying.
  • Single Tub: Basic washing machine for clothes washing.

Beyond the basic wash functions, look out for these popular features that can improve performance, convenience, and efficiency.

  • Inverter Motor: Allows the motor to run at variable speeds, making the machine quieter and more energy-saving compared to traditional motors.
  • Child Lock Safety: Essential for homes with young children.
  • Quick Wash Cycles: Perfect for lightly soiled clothes when you're in a hurry.
  • Steam Wash: Uses steam to tackle allergies and loosen tough stains.
  • Smart/WiFi Controls: Allows you to start, stop, or monitor the wash cycle remotely via a smartphone app.
  • Drum Material: Look for a stainless steel drum as it's more durable and resists rust and odors better than plastic or porcelain.
  • Front-load machines: 1–2 teaspoons of HE (High Efficiency) detergent. This type is low-sudsing and specially formulated for the low water levels used in front-loaders.
  • Top-load machines: 1–2 tablespoons of regular detergent (unless otherwise stated). Important: Follow the manufacturer’s instructions to use the correct amount of detergent. Using too much can leave residue and excess foam, which may damage the machine over time.
