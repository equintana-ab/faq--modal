To maximize the life span of your cooking range, regular maintenance is necessary to ensure that it is running in optimal conditions and remains safe to use.

Type of Cooking Range Maintenance Tips
Gas
  • Burners: Remove and clean with warm soapy water weekly. Use a soft brush to clear clogged holes.
  • Igniters: Keep them dry. If ignition fails, gently brush away debris.
  • Grates: Soak in warm water and scrub with a non-abrasive pad.
  • Gas Supply: Turn off the main valve when not in use for long periods.
Hotplate
  • Cool Before Cleaning: Wait until coils are completely cool.
  • Drip Pans: Remove and wash regularly to prevent buildup.
  • Avoid Water Contact on Coils: Wipe with a damp cloth only; do not submerge.
  • Inspect for Rust or Cracks: Replace damaged coils immediately.
Vitro Ceramic
  • Use Proper Cookware: Flat-bottomed pots for full contact.
  • Clean with Ceramic Cleaner: Use a soft scraper or ceramic cooktop cleaner for burnt stains.
  • Avoid Sugar Spills: Wipe immediately—melted sugar can permanently damage the glass.
  • Do Not Drop Pots: The glass surface can crack if hit.
Induction
  • Use Induction-Compatible Cookware: Only magnetic-bottom pots and pans.
  • Keep Surface Clean and Dry: Induction works best when no residue is between the pot and glass.
  • Avoid Scratching: Lift cookware instead of sliding it.
  • Fan Area: Keep vents free from dust for proper cooling.
